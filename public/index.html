Carbon Emissions Analogy

Imagine lifting an entire mountain into the sky. Now, pulverize the mountain and turn it into dust. Take that dust and spread it over the entire planet over the next 6 years. Next, convert all that dust into carbon dioxide. This represents how much CO2 we are emitting into the atmosphere.

The earth's temperature has been increasing yearly, especially in the last 100 years. This is due to many changing factors that our world has experienced in that time. Technology has been improving at a rapid rate the last Century with that causing vast amounts of carbon emissions.

Carbon footprints are the amount of carbon dioxide and other carbon compounds emitted due to the consumption of fossil fuels by a particular person, group, etc. There are many online resources to track your personal carbon footprint, literally just a google search away! It's important to understand our Carbon Footprints so we have the ability to improve and use Cleaner Energy.

There are many forms of clean energy. Through understanding our carbon footprints we can find areas that we can improve on as individuals and as groups. If you would like to learn more about clean energy, Energy.gov has some amazing learning resources.
